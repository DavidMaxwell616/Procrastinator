New York - The peevish hells of every day existence:

Global melting pot emits toxic odors. Free enterprise of the panhandler. Do all beggars have HIV? Subway evangelists. Who invited all the tourists?

If any of you Speak Japanese, French, Spanish, Chinese or any of the languages being muttered over the incessant honking of cab horns, please feel free to inform these tourists that the places upon which they are standing, looking up at buildings, turning around in circles like dizzy weebles, are known in America as sidewalks. Please point out that the second half of that term implies motion, usually forward with some predetermined destination. And although it could be argued that spinning around gazing at building tops indeed implies motion, I doubt that the presentation of that argument would stop me from trying to will these human pedestrian barriers into tne path of a crazed taxi driver. Can I still call them cab drivers? PC, you know. I think they might be public transportation engineers these days.

If you can finally get through the damn tourists, be prepared to be next assaulted by some enterprising panhandler, pleading with you for some spare change so that they might eat today. It's an emotional moment, until the cheap rye is detected on the breath. The unfortunate bastard in front of you assures you that he is HIV positive and has been unable to receive help from the government. The lying bastard reeks of booze and he's accosting me on my way to the job I despise, for which I trudge my way through these bastards five days a week so that I might afford to catch the buzz this leech is sporting at nine in the morning. Of course it never occurs to me at the moment of confrontation to ask how many times he shot up with dirty needles before he contracted the deadly disease.

You've finally gotten away from the beggar without him spitting on you, just in time for the walk sign to turn deep red as you step down into the street to cross. It chuckles at you as it grins from ear to ear with "DON'T WALK". You look at your watch to see how long it's taken you to walk that brutal half a block, when you are shoved from behind, almost falling in front of a quick moving cab. You turn around to see a rather unapologetic swine with fresh out of the box roller blades laced to his feet. The fucker is learning how to roller blade during rush hour on a busy sidewalk. Par for the course, he can hardly stand, never mind stop on his own accord. But what the hell, if he wasn't on the sidewalk, flailing around accosting pedestrians, he'd be in the road, taking up a lane of traffic and causing the harried anxiety of a feeding frenzy among the motorists.

The smart ass crossing post finally has annoyed you enougn and turns green. You are almost there. The entrance to the subway is close. You'll finally be underground, away from the insanity of the street.
