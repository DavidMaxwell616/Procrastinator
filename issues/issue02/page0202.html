The steps in front of the Callahassee courthouse were densely packed with the curious and the furious this morning, as The Mexican Mud Band held a press conference to apologise for luring mudheads across the nation into divulging their favorite Chinese foodstuffs. Howls of disapproval and outrage greeted them as they took the platform and began cockily dissembling the equipment. Scott Lehr blearily denied charges that the band was attempting to sell the results and admitted with regret that they believed El Della Rucho was attempting to 'fix' the poll, somehow, for whatever reason of his own. The crowd disbanded dazedly after the Mud launched an improvisatory a capella rap that threatened to last long into the night.

Della Rucho was unavailable for comment. Three microphones found missing. Mud believed reponsible.

THE ORIGINS OF THE MUD (chapter 2)

In a little log dorm in the Syracuse Arctic, the anguished cries of a newborn baby band are heard. Soon the distraught, alcohol-adddled parents hear a knock at the door-BOOM! BOOM! A heartless Resident Advisor bursts in and complains that the baby is too loud. The RA, with a cruel glint in her eye, threatens to 'write" the parents up if they don't smother the child or at least move it down to the basement linen closet. Being high slackers, the parents almost consider the former option, but compassion prevails and the newborn family begins its long, torturous journey from the soft, smoky decadence of the seventh floor to the dank. cold starkness of the linen closet. There, as fate would have it, the baby can actually be heard by more people, as its caterwalling echoes up the 22 story elevator shaft of Lawrinson Hall. Looks of fear

and panic cross the formerly complacent college student elevator passengers. The baby had begun to speak.
This was back before funk had re-ignited the stove of music, back when the general populace was numbed by mid-80's Top 40 with its regurgitated pablum and plywood complexity-The young band was forced to wean itself on the damp mulch and cold gruel of the Dylanesque 60's. Knowing few chords, fewer beats and saddled with any drifting "musicians" available, the young Mud wailed and screeched its blues away, straining to be heard through shitty amps and broken drums. As It creeped along in its grotesquely disfigured, but well-intentioned state, no one would have guessed that such chaotic squalor would eventually evolve into such a fierce, adamant and complex creature as the TRUE MUD!!
