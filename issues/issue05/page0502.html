TRACY & PATTY'S COOKING CORNER

"When you wake up in the morning with your head on fire and your eyes too bloody to see, you can cry in your coffee but don't call me I'm asleep."

What goes up must come down, girls and few things re so high and fall so hard as a good solid alcohol buzz. My strategy to deal with hangovers involves first treating the symptoms (Advil for headaches, marijuana for nausea, and sex if the only thing to help is to take your mind off your suffering) Stretching is a good idea, too, as are Woody Allen movies, especially Stardust Memories and Play It Again Sam. I know you're not supposed to, but eating a cheeseburger

before passing out the night before always works for me. I also find that the mental and physical pain of hangover is lessened when the drinks that brought it on were free and of high quality. For the serious hangover that calls for the serious cure, try this recipe, honed in the kitchen of master chef Patty Headstrong:

MINESTRONE A LA MEASY

-4 ounces salt pork rind removed
-2 tablespoons unsalted butter
-4 cloves garlic, finely minced
-2 carrots, peeled and diced
-1 onion, peeled end diced

-2 zucchini, diced
-1 Idaho potato, peeled and diced
-4 cups chicken broth or canned broth
-2 cups homemade beef stock or broth
-1 large can whole peeled tomatoes
-5 tablespoons fresh parsley
-2 teaspoons dried oregano
-1 1/2 teaspoons dried basil
-1 teaspoon ground black pepper
-Salt to taste
-1 can red kidney beans or pinto beans
-1/2 cup small pasta (small bows or ditali)
-Freshly grated parmesan cheese
-1 small can chick peas

1. Cook the salt pork in a large soup pot over low heat to render the fat about 10 minutes. Do not let it brown. Add the butter. When it has melted add the garlic, carrots, onion and leek and raise the heat slightly. Cover and wilt the vegetables for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
2. Add the zucchini, potato, both stocks, and tomatoes. Bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and add 2 tablespoons of the parsley, oregano, basil, pepper, and salt. Simmer over medium heat for 30 minute.
3. Add the beans, tomatoes and pasta. Simmer until the pasta ts tender, 10 minutes. Adjust the seasonings and heat through. Remove salt pork. Stir in the remaining parsley and serve with Parmesan cheese alongside.

SERVES 6 TO 8 PERSONS
